# Dashboard Purpose

The reminder of what this project is trying to do. Every chart, every blurb, every framework signal should earn its place against this. When in doubt — does this help the reader answer one of these six questions? If yes, keep. If it's interesting but doesn't, deep-dive page or cut.

---

## What the reader should leave with

After looking at the overview page, the reader should be able to answer six plain-English questions about Canada's macro state, framed as the Bank of Canada itself frames them:

| Section | Sub-question the reader should leave with |
|---|---|
| Monetary Policy | What is the BoC doing right now? |
| Inflation | Is the BoC's mandate (the 2% target) being met? |
| GDP & Activity | Is the economy at potential? |
| Labour | Is there slack? How tight is the labour market? |
| Housing | Is the most rate-sensitive sector amplifying or dampening policy? |
| Financial Conditions | What external winds are pushing on inflation, growth, CAD? |

The implicit bigger question those six compose into is roughly: *where does Canada stand relative to the BoC's goals, and what's likely to shape the next policy decision?* But that framing is fuzzier than the six sub-questions; the six are the working surface.

## Status

This doc is a v0 working draft. The sub-questions are not signed off as final — they're the explicit version of what was previously implicit in the dashboard's section structure. Expect revisions as the questions get clearer, especially on:

- Whether high-level plain-English questions are being missed on the overview (the six sections answer their sub-question but a page-level synthesis is currently absent)
- Whether the trade / investment / fiscal / demographics dimensions belong on the overview, on deep-dive pages, or out of scope
- How deep-dive pages relate to overview sections — one-to-one, or different cuts

---

## Filter

When considering a chart, indicator, or framework signal:

- **Earns its place on the overview** — directly helps the reader answer the section's sub-question, in the cleanest form. The wage band tells the wage-growth story; the four individual wage measures' dispersion is detail beneath that.
- **Belongs on a deep-dive page** — useful to a reader who wants to drill into a specific section, but redundant or distracting on the overview. CPI components, GDP industry decomposition, BoC balance sheet composition.
- **Out of scope** — interesting but doesn't map to any of the six questions. Park or skip.

Bloat is what happens when individually-reasonable additions accrete without this filter. The dashboard is meant to be clear, not comprehensive.
